Research direction

From paper fragments to academic hypergraphs

My current work explores how to extract meaningful research entities from papers, learn their association space, and generate hyperedges that represent multi-entity scholarly relations.

01

Framework extraction

Parse fragments into research roles such as problem, gap, method, evidence, claim, and limitation.

02

Entity modeling

Build entity records from original text so the model learns from complete research units.

03

Association learning

Use concepts, academic roles, shared anchors, and hard negatives to learn association vectors.

04

Hyperedge generation

Group complementary entities into traceable hyperedges that explain research directions.

Featured prototype

PaperHyperGraphRAG

A research prototype for constructing HyperGraphRAG-style academic hypergraphs from paper fragments or existing entity JSON.

What it does

Repository

PaperHyperGraphRAG converts academic text into nested frameworks, meaningful entities, association graphs, and generated hyperedges. The emphasis is not keyword matching, but functional academic relations such as limitation-method, claim-evidence, and method-evidence.

GraphRAG Hypergraph Entity extraction Research tooling Python
Association vectors Represent entity-level academic relation space.
Top-k neighbors Expose closely related entities with traceable scores.
Generated hyperedges Capture higher-order relationships across papers.
